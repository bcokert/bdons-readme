**The two documented options (per PITFALLS.md Pitfall 1 and CONTEXT.md D-11):**

**Option A — Behavioral anchor (keep the spirit of the claim, attach a falsifiable observation):**

Pair the claim with a concrete behavioral fact a report can verify against observed behavior. The anchor turns an unverifiable claim into a falsifiable one.

Proposed wording:

```markdown
* Flexibility - I update my position when new data lands. The signal you'll see: I'll say "I changed my mind on X, here's why" in a 1:1 or thread rather than quietly rewriting the plan. If you give me data that should change my mind and it doesn't, call it out — that's the failure mode.
```

Voice check: ~50 words vs. current ~22; the lengthening is justified by Pitfall 1 (the fix is the entire point). Distinctive markers (terse declaratives, blunt phrasing) preserved. Removes "love" (aspirational-emotion word on the QUAL-02 trust-claim audit list) and "never" (the actual flagged word). Introduces a falsifiable behavior ("I'll say 'I changed my mind on X'") and an explicit failure mode (the report can call it out).

**Option B — Softened claim (lose the trust-claim energy entirely, keep voice short):**

Drop "love" and "never" without adding a behavioral anchor. The bullet becomes shorter, blunter, and makes a weaker claim — but a verifiable one.

Proposed wording:

```markdown
* Flexibility - I try to hold opinions loosely and update on new data. If you give me data that should change my mind and it doesn't, call it out.
```

Voice check: ~28 words, close to current length. Removes both flagged words. Adds the failure-mode line for the same reason as Option A — without it, "try to hold opinions loosely" is still soft-aspirational. Closer to the existing voice's terseness.

**Recommendation (per D-11): Option A — behavioral anchor.**

BUT — Bdon owns the voice. If he prefers brevity over the anchor, Option B is valid.
